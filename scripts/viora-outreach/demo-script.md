# VIORA DEMO CALL SCRIPT — 15 MINUTES TO CLOSE

> **Rule #1:** Listen more than you talk. They talk 60%, you talk 40%.
> **Rule #2:** Never pitch before you understand their pain.
> **Rule #3:** After asking for money — shut up. First one to talk loses.

---

## BEFORE THE CALL (2 min prep)
- [ ] Their website open — know their name, services, hours
- [ ] Demo audio ready (barbershop + HVAC recording)
- [ ] Booking link + invoice ready to send
- [ ] Their industry pain point loaded (see cheat sheet at bottom)

---

## OPENING — 0:00 to 0:30 (Qualify + Set Agenda)

> "Hey [Name], appreciate you jumping on. I know you're busy so I'll keep this tight — 15 minutes. I'll show you how the AI receptionist works, you tell me if it makes sense for [Business Name]. Sound good?"

**→ Wait for yes. Sets the frame: this is a decision call, not an info call.**

---

## DISCOVERY — 0:30 to 3:30 (Find The Pain)

Ask these three questions. Let them talk.

**Question 1 — Current state:**
> "How are you handling incoming calls right now at [Business Name]?"

**Question 2 — The gap:**
> "What happens when nobody can pick up — after hours, busy times, lunch?"

**Question 3 — The cost:**
> "When someone calls and gets voicemail, what do they usually do?"

*(They'll say "call the next place on Google." That's the pain.)*

**Bridge to demo:**
> "Yeah, that's exactly what we solve. Let me show you — this is a 60-second recording of a real AI call."

---

## DEMO — 3:30 to 8:30 (Show, Don't Tell)

> "I'm going to play you a recording of our AI answering for a [similar industry]. Listen to how natural it sounds."

**→ PLAY THE RECORDING.** Match their industry if possible.

**After it plays — pause 3 seconds, then:**

> "That's an AI. Answers in under 2 seconds. Works 24/7 — nights, weekends, holidays."

**→ Pause again. Let them react.**

> "The barbershop we built this for was missing about 40% of their calls. First month with the AI — 70 new appointments, just from calls they were already getting."

**Now ask:**
> "What would 70 extra bookings in a month look like for [Business Name]?"

*(This makes them picture the result for THEIR business.)*

**If they're engaged, add:**
> "And a fitness center we work with cut their front desk workload in half. Their receptionist actually helps clients in person now instead of being chained to the phone."

---

## CLOSE — 8:30 to 12:00 (Tie Pain → Solution → Price → Ask)

**Step 1 — Tie it back to THEIR pain:**
> "So earlier you mentioned [repeat their specific pain — e.g., 'you miss calls after 5pm']. This solves that completely."

**Step 2 — Anchor the price:**
> "A receptionist costs $3,000 to $6,000 a month. Works 9-5. Calls in sick. Eventually quits."
>
> "This is $2,500 one-time setup, then $350 a month. Works 24/7. Never misses. Never quits."
>
> "Most clients make the setup fee back in their first two weeks from calls they used to miss."

**→ SHUT UP. Let the math sit for 5 seconds.**

**Step 3 — Ask for the close:**
> "I can have this built and live for [Business Name] within a week. We split the setup 50/50 — $1,250 to start, $1,250 when it goes live."
>
> "Want to get started?"

**→ SHUT UP. Wait. Do not speak first.**

---

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "I need to think about it"
> "Totally fair. What's the main thing you'd be thinking about — is it the price, or something else?"

*(Get the REAL objection. Handle that one specifically.)*

If they're still hesitant:
> "How about this — I'll send you the demo recording so you can listen again. What day this week works to reconnect? I'll put 10 minutes on the calendar."

*(Book the follow-up NOW. Don't leave it open-ended.)*

---

### "It's too expensive"
> "I hear you. Quick question — how many calls do you miss per week?"

*(They'll say a number.)*

> "And what's an average customer worth to you?"

*(They'll say $100-$2,000.)*

> "So if the AI catches even 2-3 of those a month, it's paid for 10x over. Most owners are spending $350 to make $2,000-$5,000 they were leaving on the table."

---

### "We already have someone answering phones"
> "That's great — this isn't about replacing them. It's about covering the gaps: after hours, weekends, when they're on another call, when they call in sick."
>
> "They work together. Your person handles walk-ins and complex stuff. The AI handles everything else."

---

### "I'm not sure it'll work for my business"
> "What specifically are you worried about?"

*(Listen. Then address it.)*

> "Tell you what — let me build a custom demo just for [Business Name]. You'll hear exactly how it would sound for YOUR customers. If you don't like it, no charge. Fair?"

---

### "Can I see it work with my own business first?"
> "Absolutely. That's what the setup fee covers — I build a fully custom AI receptionist for [Business Name]. If it's not right, we tweak it until it is."
