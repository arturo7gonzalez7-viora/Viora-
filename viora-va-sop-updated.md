## SECTION 8: HOW TO FIND VALID OWNER EMAILS (NO BOUNCES)

**PRIORITY ORDER (try in this order until you find one):**

### Method 1: LinkedIn Email (BEST — Use This First)
1. Find owner on LinkedIn
2. Look for email in their "Contact Info" section
3. If visible, use that email (it's usually their personal/business email)
4. Add new column in Google Sheets: "Email Source" = LinkedIn

### Method 2: Hunter.io / Apollo.io (SECOND BEST)
1. Go to hunter.io (free: 25 searches/month) or apollo.io
2. Enter: Business domain + Owner name
3. Copy verified email
4. Mark "Email Source" = Hunter

### Method 3: Email Pattern Guess + Verify (LAST RESORT)
Common patterns for small businesses:
- [firstname]@businessdomain.com
- [firstname][lastname]@businessdomain.com
- [firstinitial][lastname]@businessdomain.com

**Example:** John Smith at abcroofing.com
- john@abcroofing.com
- johnsmith@abcroofing.com
- jsmith@abcroofing.com

**Verify using:** emailhippo.com or neverbounce.com (free tier)

### Method 4: Call and Ask (When All Else Fails)
1. Call business number
2. Say: "Hi, I'm trying to reach [Owner Name] via email. What's the best email address?"
3. Log the email in sheet

### BOUNCE PREVENTION RULE (MANDATORY)
Before adding ANY lead to the sheet:
- Test the email using emailhippo.com (paste email, click verify)
- Only add if status = "Valid" or "Catchall"
- NEVER add if status = "Invalid" or "Unknown"

**If you can't find a valid email after trying all 4 methods:**
- DO NOT add the lead
- Move to next business
